Mobile Network Giant O2 Fined £10.5m for Overcharging Customers

Shamal Harave

With an estimated 27 million subscribers, O2 UK is the second-largest mobile network operator in the UK. Many other smaller companies use the O2 network (GiffGaff, Sky Mobile, Tesco Mobile and Lycamobile), making it a well-respected provider to choose. However, a study by the telecommunications regulator Ofcom showed shocking evidence that for around eight years, some customers who were leaving the network were double charged some fees on their final bills! This cataclysmic error affected over 140,000 pay monthly subscribers totalling up to an enormous £2.4 million!

In return, O2 said that it had refunded the majority of the customers, adding an extra 4% to the sum involved. Still, a small segment of former subscribers have not been able to be contacted and these funds will be donated to charity. On top of the 140,000 who ended up paying the mysterious extra amount, a further 110,000 people were overcharged a total of £38m - but didn’t end up paying the bill and weren’t due a refund.

Despite all of the refunds given out by O2, Ofcom said in their report that “O2 had broken important rules around providing customers with accurate bills and that it deserved a fine.”

Alarmingly, O2 had apparently known about the billing issue in 2011, but failed attempts to fix it let do the scandal continuing for eight more years! O2 were “disappointed by the technical error” and they “sincerely apologise to the customers impacted.”

Subsequently, O2 changed their billing process and after Ofcom inspection, it has been ensured that it will never happen again.

Ofcom’s enforcement chief, Gaucho Rasmussen said that the incident is “A serious breach of our rules and this fine of £10.5m is a reminder that we will step in if we see companies failing to protect their customers.”
